Global Leaders (Best for Community UX)
HotUKDeals (UK) / Pepper.pl (Poland): These are part of the Pepper.com network. Their UI is much more modern than OzBargain. They use a "Degrees" system (Hot/Cold) instead of simple Up/Down votes.

Slickdeals (USA): Excellent for "Featured Deals" and expert editorial curation. Look at their "Deal Alerts" system.

Dealabs (France): Great mobile app experience.

Niche & Local (Best for Australia context)
PriceHipster: Great for price history graphs. Integrating a "Price History" feature is the #1 way to beat OzBargain's "is this actually a deal?" skepticism.

TopBargains: A direct competitor to OzBargain but focuses more on curated coupons.

Buckscoop: Good for seeing how to integrate "Cashback" (like ShopBack/Cashrewards) into a deal site.

# Market Comparison

| Feature Area | OzBargain (Community) | Modern Peers (Automation) | Your Intersection (Sweet Spot) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Submission** | Manual, slow, high-friction | Scrapers, fast, sometimes spammy | Smart Scraper: Paste a link → auto-fill metadata → user just adds "why it's a good deal" |
| **Trust** | Heavy moderation & Karma | Algorithmic "Trending" feeds | Weighted Momentum: deals move up based on *who* is voting, not just vote count |
| **Discovery** | Chronological (river of news) | ML-driven "For You" feeds | Hybrid Feed: "New" tab for purists + "Personalized" tab for casual users |
| **Validation** | Users arguing in comments | Basic price comparison | Live Price History: embedded graphs (like CamelCamelCamel) that verify the deal instantly |
| **Interaction** | Forum-style threaded text | App-first, haptic, visual | T3 Stack PWA: native-feeling web app with swipe-to-vote and real-time live counters |
